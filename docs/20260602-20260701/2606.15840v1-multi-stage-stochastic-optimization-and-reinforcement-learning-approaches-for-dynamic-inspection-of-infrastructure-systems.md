## 摘要
我们研究基础设施系统中的一个动态检测问题，其中多辆车被调度和路径规划，以监测受异质性和随机故障影响的组件。一个关键挑战是内源性不确定性，其中故障时间实现通过先前的检测行动和故障传播动态进行过滤。这产生了一个多阶段决策问题，整合了路径规划、调度和随时间变化的决策依赖的可靠性动态。我们将该问题建模为一个多阶段随机混合整数规划，在内源性不确定性下联合优化路径规划和调度决策。我们开发了一种随机对偶动态整数规划（SDDiP）算法，该算法集成对偶逼近、整数状态约简和基于采样的前向模拟来近似成本函数。同时，我们提出了一种强化学习框架，通过与模拟系统动态和故障过程的交互来学习作业聚类结构和路径规划策略。在具有不同拓扑和故障模式的基础设施网络上的数值实验表明，SDDiP能产生高质量解但面临可扩展性限制，而基于学习的方法在具有竞争力的性能下实现了强大的可扩展性，突出了最优性与可处理性之间的权衡。这项工作推进了多阶段随机路径规划问题中内源性不确定性的建模，并桥接了随机规划与基于学习的方法。研究结果为何时部署基于优化与基于学习的方法提供了指导，从而在实践中有助于更有效和可扩展的检测规划。更广泛地，该框架支持风险感知的检测资源分配，并提高关键基础设施系统的可靠性。

## Abstract
We study a dynamic inspection problem for infrastructure systems in which multiple vehicles are routed and scheduled to monitor components subject to heterogeneous and stochastic failures. A key challenge is endogenous uncertainty in which failure-time realizations are filtered through prior inspection actions and failure-propagation dynamics. This yields a multi-stage decision problem that integrates routing, scheduling, and decision-dependent reliability dynamics over time. We formulate the problem as a multi-stage stochastic mixed-integer program that jointly optimizes routing and scheduling decisions under endogenous uncertainty. We develop a stochastic dual dynamic integer programming (SDDiP) algorithm that integrates dual approximation, integer state reduction, and sampling-based forward simulation to approximate cost-to-go functions. In parallel, we propose a reinforcement learning framework that learns job clustering structures and routing policies through interaction with simulated system dynamics and failure processes. Numerical experiments on infrastructure networks with diverse topologies and failure patterns show that SDDiP yields high-quality solutions but faces scalability limitations, while the learning-based approach achieves strong scalability with competitive performance, highlighting a trade-off between optimality and tractability. This work advances the modeling of endogenous uncertainty in multi-stage stochastic routing problems and bridges stochastic programming with learning-based approaches. The results provide guidance on when to deploy optimization-based versus learning-based methods, enabling more effective and scalable inspection planning in practice. More broadly, the framework supports risk-aware allocation of inspection resources and improved reliability of critical infrastructure systems.
